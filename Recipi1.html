Gulab Jamun:


Ingredients


  • Sugar syrup
  • Milk
  • Flour
  • Oil

Recipi:


  1. Combine sugar and water in a pan and bring to a boil to create the syrup. Remove from heat, add cardamom, and set aside to cool.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, paneer, sooji, Nestlé MILKMAID, baking powder, and baking soda. Mix gently to form a soft dough, avoiding over-kneading
  3. Divide the dough into 30-35 portions and gently shape them into round balls.
  4. Heat oil on low flame and fry the balls until they turn golden brown.
  5. Transfer the fried Gulab Jamuns into the cooled sugar syrup. Once all the Gulab Jamuns are added, bring the syrup to a boil again briefly, then remove from heat.
  6. Enjoy your homemade Gulab Jamun warm, garnished with your favourite toppings.


Rasmalai:


Ingredients


  • chana (cheese patties)
  • Rabri(milk based sauce)
  • Sugar Syrup
  • Elichi


Recipi:


  1. Sour the whole milk with lemon juice.
  2. Discard all the water (whey) and put solids (curds) in cheese cloth and press it with heavy weight.
  3. After 1/2 hour take out the weight and knead the chenna very properly.
  4. Make small balls of the size of dime.
  5. In a sauce pan boil 3 cups of water and sugar together.
  6. When it starts to boil, drop in the chenna balls.
  7. On medium heat cover the pan and let it simmer for 1/2 an hour. If you have Indian style pressure cooker keep the lid closed with whistle on for 15 minutes on a VERY LOW HEAT(extremely important). switch off the heat.Frequently stir it so that the chenna balls don't stick to each other.
  8. In another pan boil heavy cream, 2 cups of whole milk and saffron strands. Add 5 tablespoon of sugar.
  9. Keep simmering for 20 minutes until the milk becomes thickened, taking care not to scorch.
  10. Now take out the chenna balls from the sugar syrup.
  11. While the whipping cream and whole milk is still simmering, drop in the chenna balls in it and simmer for another 15 minutes on low heat.
  12. place the milk rasamalai in a bowl, sprinkle cardamom powder and almond flakes and refrigerate.
  13. Serve cold.


Kaju Katli:


Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups (250 g) whole raw cashew nuts
  2. 2 tablespoons (15 g) whole milk powder
  3. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder.
  4. 10 tablespoons plus 1 1/2 teaspoons (125 g) granulated sugar.
  5. Rounded 1/2 teaspoon ghee.
  6. Edible silver leaf or vark, optional.


Recipi:


  1. Place 1 cup cashews (kaju) in a dry grinder or coffee grinder or blender
  2. Grind the kaju to a smooth powder, making sure you don’t over do the grinding process as fat should not release from the cashews.
  3. Combine ½ cup sugar and 5 tablespoons water in a thick bottomed pan (or kadai or a non-stick pan).
  4. Keep on low flame and let the sugar dissolve in the water. There is no consistency in the sugar syrup like one string or two string. Simply let the sugar dissolve in the water and then proceed with the next step.
  5. When the sugar completely dissolves in the water, add the cashew powder.
  6. Stir and keep on stirring non-stop on a low flame. If there are lumps then break the lumps as you stir and continue to stir. This consistent stirring is the key for a smooth and even kaju katli dough.
  7. The mixture will thicken and start coming together to form a very soft dough. Then it is time to remove the whole lump of the mixture from the pan and place it on a work surface.
  8. Put the entire cashew dough on a work surface. Add 1 teaspoon rose petals (or 1 teaspoon rose water or 8 to 9 strands of saffron) and 1 tablespoon ghee or coconut oil.
  9. Knead the dough lightly, flatten it and place it on a greased plate or tray. Don't overdo kneading as this will release fat from the cashews.
  10. You can also place it on a large piece of parchment or butter paper. The cashew dough should still be quite warm when you start rolling it
  11. Once cooled completely, cut the cashew dough into squares or diamond shapes. Scrape off the uneven edges and enjoy as a little snack as you work.
